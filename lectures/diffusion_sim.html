Monte Carlo methods

Monte Carlo methods use randomness and statistical estimators to solve a variety of problems:
  • calculations of complicated expressions, eg, multi-variate integrals
  • finding approximate solutions of optimization or other problems that are intractable by algorithms with polynomial scaling (eg, can be NP-hard)
  • sampling/generating possible scenarios in uncertainty and error propagation studies
  • using stochastic processes to simulate processes that can be stochastic, deterministic or a mix of both.

Diffusion equation

    • Fick's law in 1D:
      flux $f$ is a function of change in density $n$ (concentration), empirical observation

      $$ f(x,t) = -D_0 {\partial n(x,t) \over \partial x}$$

    • Continuity equation, mass preservation:
      change in density over time at a location = inflow and outflow of material at this location

      $$ {\partial n(x,t) \over \partial t} + {\partial f(x,t) \over \partial x} = 0 $$

    • Diffusion equation:
      change in density over time at location $x$ is proportional to change in gradient at this location

      $$ {\partial n(x,t) \over \partial t} = D_0 {\partial^2 n(x,t) \over \partial x^2} $$

    • $f(x,t)$ is flux, $n(x,t)$ is density, $x$ is location and $t$ is time, D_0 is diffusion constant

Fundamental solution

  • Given a unit impuls $\delta(x-x_0)=n(x,t=0)$ the fundamental solution of diffusion equation is

  • $$ n(x,t) = {1 \over \sqrt{4\pi D_0 t}} e^{{-(x-x_0)^2} \over ({4D_0t)}}$$

  • a Gaussian with $ N(x_0, \sigma^2 = 2D_0t) $

Green's function for diffusion

    We can rewrite the diffusion equation using Green's function

  • $$n({\bf r},t+\tau)=\int G_0({\bf r},{\bf r}',\tau)n({\bf r}',t) d{\bf r}'$$
  • which is evolution equation

  • and where the Green's function for diffusion equation is the fundamental solution $$ G_d({\bf r},{\bf r}', \tau) = {1 \over \sqrt{4\pi D_0 \tau}} e^{{-|{\bf r}-{\bf r}'|^2} \over {4D_0\tau}}$$

Stochastic method of solution

    For complex processes which cannot be solved analytically we need numerical solution

  • $$n({\bf r},t+\tau)=\int G_0({\bf r},{\bf r}',\tau)n({\bf r}',t) d{\bf r}'$$
  • which is evolution equation

  • $${\bf r}^{(i)}(\tau)={\bf r}^{(i)}(0)+\vec\eta^{(i)}$$
  • where $\eta \elem N(0, \sigma^2 = 2D_0\tau)$
