Geostatistical simulations: motivation

Derive model of spatial distribution of an (environmental) variable based on a limited set of discrete observations:

  • if the measured data capture the features of the distribution at the level of detail needed for the application we can use interpolation See GIS582 Spatial interpolation topic
  • if the measured data are limited and we know statistical properties of the distribution, we can use simulation
  • some interpolation methods are based on deterministic, process-based simulations: splines

Geostatistical simulations: motivation

Estimation versus simulation

  • we are modeling complex distribution (e.g. surface)
  • limited number of samples is available - interpolation does not capture the complexity, the result is too smooth
  • we know the statistical properties of the complex distribution
  • we generate many realizations of the surface with the given stats properties
  • the realizations are used to compute the simulated complex distribution and its uncertainty

Geostatistical simulations: applications

Observations where sampling is limited:

  • subsurface: reservoir modeling, mining, soil properties, groundwater pollution
  • surface: variables not detectable by RS, e.g. some pollutants
  • uncertainty propagation studies
