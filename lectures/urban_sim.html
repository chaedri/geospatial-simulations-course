Cellular automata principle

Space is tesselated into grid cells $(i,j)$ (usually square) with initial state $C_{i,j}^{t=0}$

General expression for the cell state evolution: $$ C_{ij}^{t+1} = F(C_{ij}^{t}, O_{ij}^{t}, R) $$ State of cell $(i,j)$ at time $t+1$ is a function of

  • its state at time $t$,
  • state of the cells in its neighborhood or more general impact of its neighborhood $O_{ij}^{t}$
  • transition rules $R$

Game of life example

Rules:

  • Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbours dies, as if by underpopulation.
  • Any live cell with two or three live neighbours lives on to the next generation.
  • Any live cell with more than three live neighbours dies, as if by overpopulation.
  • Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbours becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.
For the given rules, the resulting pattern is dependent only on the initial configuration: example of emergence and self-organization

Geospatial cellular automata

  • initial state is based on land use / land cover data, usually from RS or GIS
  • rules are derived from socio-economic data
  • models can be calibrated and validated used past time series

Probabilistic CA urban growth models

  • Probabilistic transition rules and a stochastic perturbation term
  • Statistical methods are used to develop the transition rules, based on past time series
  • Logistic regression, neural networks, decision trees
New states are generated based on a probabilistic distribution
